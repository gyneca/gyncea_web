Managing Team

Anisha Prakash

Co-Editor in chief, Founder

Anisha Prakash is a junior at Barnard College of Columbia University, pursuing a Women's, Gender, and Sexuality studies with a Chemistry minor. Last summer, she worked as a Summer Undergraduate Research Fellow at the Dietrich Lab at Columbia, conducting microbiology and genetics research on Pseudomonas aeruginosa, a bacteria that causes infections in people with cystic fibrosis and burn wounds. Having studied feminist theory and experiencing the struggles of being a woman in science, she is passionate about fighting the gender disparities in medicine on both a scientific and social level. She’s excited to work with Gyneca’s talented team to publish its first issue.

Ashley Liang

Co-Editor in chief

Ashley Liang is a junior at Columbia University studying Biochemistry. She oversees the Web Development department of GYNECA. As an aspiring female scientist currently pursuing research at the Sternberg Lab in Columbia’s Medical Center campus, women’s health is directly at the center of her academic and social interests. At GYNECA, she hopes to not only help showcase other undergraduate researchers, but also foster a community in which knowledge of women’s health is both expanded and celebrated. Outside of her educational pursuits, she enjoys golfing and watching The Great British Bake Off.

Ananya Raghavan

Executive Editor

Ananya Raghavan is a sophomore in Columbia University's School of Engineering and Applied Science (SEAS). She oversees Art and the Social Media departments of GYNECA. She hopes to major in biomedical engineering on the premed track, minoring in Middle Eastern and South Asian studies. She is very interested in the intersection between medicine, art, and gender studies and hopes to explore these areas more with GYNECA. In her free time, she can be found painting, playing with her golden doodle, Copper, or curled up with a good book/Netflix bollywood film.

Tara Samsel

Executive Editor

Tara Samsel is a senior studying Molecular and Cellular Biology at Barnard College of Columbia University on the pre-medical track. She oversees the Revision Editing and Journalism departments of GYNECA. She is currently training to be an Emergency Medical Technician at Columbia University’s volunteer EMS program, CUEMS. She is an Undergraduate Research Fellow at Columbia University Fertility Center conducting her own independent project in Dr. Zev William’s embryology lab introducing automated liquid handling. Attending a historically women’s college, working in the medical field, and conducting a fertility research project has fueled her interest in women’s health and prompted her passion for health disparities in Obstetrics & Gynecology.
